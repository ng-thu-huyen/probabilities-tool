COUPON COLLECTOR

DESCRIPTION

There are multiple coupons in a collection. A collector has the ability to purchase a coupon, but can’t choose the coupon he purchases. Instead, the coupon is revealed to be coupon i with probability pi. Let N be the number of coupons he’ll need to collect before he has at least one coupon of each type. Let’s call this random variable X. Now, this tool helps you solve the following questions.

  • What is the expected value of X when the coupons have equal probabilities of being collected?
  • what is the expected value of X when the coupons have unequal probabilities of being collected?
  • What is the variance of X when the coupons have equal probabilities of being collected?
  • What is the variance of X when the coupons have unequal probabilities of being collected?
  • What is the density function of X (meaning the entire distribution) when the coupons have equal probabilities?
  • What is the density function of X (meaning the entire distribution) when the coupons have unequal probabilities?

INPUT SECTION





Probability must be larger than 0 and less than 1.
Type 1: Enter one input only.
Type 2: Enter input for each coupon on each line.

QUEUEING THEORY

DESCRIPTION

A system (e.g. banks, supermarkets, computers, public transport) has to deal with problems related to queueing (waiting). The tools helps congestion analysis in the system.
A typical system involves two subjects (1) servers, (2) customers. It is always assumed that there is just a single queue (waiting line) and customers move from this single queue to the servers.
There are different types of queing systems, whose general notation is A/S/c.

  • A: Arrival process of customers.
    In reality, most arrival process follows Markov (M) model (exponential probability density).
  • S: Server process: either
    • M (Markov): Exponential probability density
    • G (General): Any arbitrary probability distribution
  • c: Number of servers

The tools helps congestion analysis in the system by answers to the following questions.

  • What is the mean customer inter-arrival rate?
  • What is the mean service rate?
  • What is the variance of inter-arrival time?
  • What is the variance of service time?
  • What is the probability that the server is busy?
  • What is the mean number of customers in the queue?
  • What is the mean waiting time in the queue?
  • What is the mean waiting time in the system
  • What is the mean number of customers in the system?
  • What is the probability that the server is idle?

INPUT SECTION





The value must be larger than 0.


The value must be larger than 0 and less than mean customer inter-arrival time. Or your queue would be infinite.

The value must be larger than 0 and minimum service time, and less than mean customer inter-arrival time. Or your queue would be infinite.
